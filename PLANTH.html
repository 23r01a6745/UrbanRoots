  • Phytopathology

    Plant diseases, detrimental to plant health, result from pathogens, environmental stressors, or physiological imbalances. Phytopathology is the study of these diseases, their causes, diagnosis, and management. It aids in understanding disease development, spread, and control, fostering healthier plants and sustainable agriculture.

  • Preventive Measures

    Preventing plant diseases is essential for maintaining healthy gardens. Ensuring good air circulation by proper spacing and pruning reduces humidity and helps prevent fungal diseases. Wise watering practices, such as watering at the base and using drip irrigation, keep foliage dry and minimize fungal growth. Choosing disease-resistant plant varieties reduces the likelihood of infection. Maintaining cleanliness by regularly removing dead or diseased plant material and sterilizing tools prevents the spread of pathogens. Applying fungicides preventively during high-risk periods protects plants from fungal infections. Implementing these measures helps keep gardens healthy and thriving.
